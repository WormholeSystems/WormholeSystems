# Mass

A wormhole's **mass** is how much total ship mass it can still pass before it collapses. Every ship that jumps through reduces it. You set the status as heavy ships cross — the app doesn't measure mass for you.

| Status       | Mass remaining                                                                                         |
| ------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Fresh**    | Over ~50% remaining. Plenty of room.                                                                   |
| **Reduced**  | Under ~50% remaining. Be deliberate about what you send through.                                       |
| **Critical** | Under ~10% remaining. A single large ship may close it — and could strand whatever is on the far side. |
| **Unknown**  | Not yet assessed.                                                                                      |

The percentages reflect EVE's wormhole mechanics and match the in-game shrink stages ("has had its stability reduced", "is reaching the end of its natural lifetime"). When you see a hole shrink in-game, drop its status down a level here.

> Mass is independent of [lifetime](/documentation/connections/lifetime). Watch both before committing a fleet.

Autopilot can avoid reduced or critical holes — see [Wormhole filters](/documentation/autopilot-and-routing/wormhole-filters).
