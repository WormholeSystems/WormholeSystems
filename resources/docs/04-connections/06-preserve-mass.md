# Preserve mass

Sometimes you want to keep a hole's [mass](/documentation/connections/mass) intact so it's still there when you need it later, a rolling exit, a staging route, or a hole you're deliberately keeping fresh. **Preserve mass** is a flag that tells everyone on the map: don't push heavy ships through this connection.

## What it does

It's a signal, nothing more. It does **not** change how mass is tracked, it does **not** stop the [mass status](/documentation/connections/mass) from updating, and it does **not** protect the connection from cleanup. It simply marks the hole so your fleet knows to treat it gently and route heavy ships elsewhere.

## Turning it on

You can toggle it from two places:

- Right-click the connection and choose **Preserve mass**.
- Open the menu on a wormhole [signature](/documentation/signatures/basics) that's linked to the connection and choose **Preserve mass** there.

Both act on the same connection, so it doesn't matter which side you set it from.

## Where you'll see it

A connection with preserve mass on shows a small heart badge on its line, and a **Preserve mass** row appears in the connection's status popup. That's your cue to take the long way around with anything heavy.
