# Watch list

The **watch list** is the set of systems you want to keep an eye on the route to. Add a system and the **Navigation** panel shows the route to it from wherever you're currently focused, recalculated live as the chain changes.

## What the panel shows

For each system on the watch list:

- the **system** (with its wormhole class or security)
- its **region**
- the number of **jumps** from your selected origin, colour-coded so distance reads at a glance — **green** for a short hop, **amber** for a medium trip, **red** for a long one

Hover a row to see the full jump-by-jump route.

## Jump counts depend on _your_ routing settings

The jump count is the length of the route the [routing engine](/documentation/autopilot-and-routing/how-routing-works) finds — and that's shaped by **your own autopilot settings**. Your route preference, wormhole [filters](/documentation/autopilot-and-routing/wormhole-filters), security penalty, and whether [EVE Scout](/documentation/autopilot-and-routing/eve-scout) is enabled all change which connections are usable and how the path is chosen.

So keep in mind:

- The **same system can show a different number of jumps** for two people looking at the same map, because they have different settings.
- If a count looks too high — or a route avoids a hole you'd happily take — **edit your routing settings** and the watch list updates immediately. See [Autopilot & routing](/documentation/autopilot-and-routing/how-routing-works) for what each setting does.

## Pinning

**Pin** the systems you care about most to keep them at the top of the list; your top pinned systems also appear as one-click quick-pick buttons for fast routing.

Because routes update live, the watch list tells you not just _where_ something is but _how to get there right now_ — and warns you the moment a collapsing hole lengthens the trip. It uses the same engine as autopilot, so you can jump straight from watching a route to [setting it in-game](/documentation/autopilot-and-routing/setting-waypoints).
